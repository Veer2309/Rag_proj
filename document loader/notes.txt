Detailed Statistics Notes (Interview + Data Science + Machine Learning)
Statistics is the science of collecting, organizing, analyzing, interpreting, and presenting data. It is one of the most important subjects in Data Science, Machine Learning, AI, and Analytics.

1. What is Statistics?
Statistics is a branch of mathematics that deals with data.
It helps answer questions like:
	•	What happened?
	•	Why did it happen?
	•	What is likely to happen next?
Example
A college wants to know the average marks of students.
Instead of asking every student individually, statistics helps summarize the information.

Types of Statistics
1. Descriptive Statistics
Descriptive statistics summarizes data.
It does NOT make predictions.
Examples
	•	Mean
	•	Median
	•	Mode
	•	Variance
	•	Standard Deviation
	•	Charts
	•	Tables
Example:
Marks:
45 55 60 70 80
Instead of writing all values,
we say
Mean = 62
This is descriptive statistics.

2. Inferential Statistics
Inferential statistics makes predictions about a population using a sample.
Example
Survey 500 voters
Predict election result for 10 million voters.

2. Population vs Sample

Example

Sample
Small subset selected from population.
Example

Why use sample?
Because studying every person is
	•	expensive
	•	time consuming
	•	sometimes impossible

19. Hypothesis Testing
Used to test claims.

Null Hypothesis (H₀)
No difference.

Alternative Hypothesis (H₁)
Difference exists.

Example
Company claims average salary = ₹50,000
H₀
Salary = ₹50,000
H₁
Salary ≠ ₹50,000

p-value
Probability that observed result happened by chance.
If
p < 0.05
Reject H₀.

Type I Error
Reject true null hypothesis.
False Positive.

Type II Error
Accept false null hypothesis.
False Negative.

20. Confidence Interval
Range in which true population parameter lies with a certain confidence.
95% Confidence Interval
Means if we repeated sampling many times, about 95% of those intervals would contain the true population parameter.

22. Central Limit Theorem (CLT)
One of the most important concepts in statistics.
It states:
Regardless of the population distribution, if the sample size is sufficiently large (typically n ≥ 30), the distribution of sample means approaches a normal distribution.
Why it's important:
	•	Enables confidence intervals and hypothesis testing.
	•	Justifies many statistical methods.
	•	Widely used in machine learning and analytics.

23. Bias vs Variance
Bias
	•	Error due to overly simple assumptions.
	•	High bias → Underfitting.
Variance
	•	Error due to sensitivity to training data.
	•	High variance → Overfitting.
A good model balances both.
